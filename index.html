§ 01 About

Built at the seam between experiment and simulation.

I'm a Master's researcher in Agricultural & Biosystems Engineering at Iowa State University, working at the Soil Machine Dynamics Laboratory in collaboration with John Deere.

My thesis — "Optimization of Dry Fertilizer Spreaders: DEM-Based Material Flow Analysis and Experimental Validation" — focuses on calibrating DEM models of urea using angle of repose, then validating them independently against hopper discharge and impact plate experiments.

The interesting part isn't the simulation alone or the experiment alone — it's wiring them together so the model actually generalizes beyond its calibration data.

§ 03 DEM Workflow

From physical fertilizer to a validated model.

  1. 01

    Material Characterization

    Particle size distribution, shape, density, friction, and coefficient of restitution measured to define input material properties.

  2. 02

    Calibration via Angle of Repose

    Static AOR experiments used to tune DEM contact parameters until the simulated heap angle matches the physical pile within tolerance.

  3. 03

    Multi-Method Validation

    Independent setups — hopper discharge and impact plate tests — verify the model generalizes beyond calibration, using percentage relative error and confidence intervals.

  4. 04

    Spreader Simulation

    Full twin spinning-disc spreader simulations predict distribution patterns and inform machine settings for uniform application.

2023 — 2026 ISU × John Deere Thesis

DEM Calibration & Multi-Method Validation of Urea

Calibrated an Altair EDEM model of urea fertilizer using angle of repose, then validated independently with hopper discharge and impact plate tests. Evaluated simulation accuracy using percentage relative error, confidence intervals, and repeatability analysis. Processed data in Python, MATLAB, and ImageJ.
