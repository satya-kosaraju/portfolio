01 // PROFILE

Engineering is the full loop: understand, design, build, measure, improve.

I work where mechanical hardware, physical testing, and computational tools meet. My strength is not one software package; it is building a reliable path from an unclear field problem to a defensible engineering answer.

At Iowa State's Soil Machine Dynamics Laboratory, I analyze agricultural machinery for industry programs using CAD, fabrication, sensors, high-speed imaging, computer vision, Python, and physics-based simulation. Current work includes quantitative wear analysis of tillage components; earlier work established a validated DEM workflow for fertilizer handling systems.

I care about traceable measurements, repeatable workflows, and outputs an engineering team can act on: a geometry change, a machine setting, a wear trend, a validated model, or an automated analysis pipeline.

02 // ENGINEERING PROCESS

From an ambiguous problem to a result the team can use.

  1. 01

    Define & measure

    Translate the field problem into measurable requirements. Establish geometry, loads, material behavior, failure modes, and a clean baseline before selecting the tool.

  2. 02

    Design & model

    Develop CAD, test fixtures, algorithms, or physics-based models at the fidelity the decision requires. Prototype early and keep assumptions visible.

  3. 03

    Build & validate

    Fabricate, instrument, and test. Use independent experiments, uncertainty, repeatability, and image-based measurements to distinguish a convincing picture from a reliable engineering result.

  4. 04

    Automate & communicate

    Convert raw measurements into reproducible Python, MATLAB, JMP, or vision workflows. Deliver trends, visuals, and recommendations that make the next engineering decision faster and clearer.

Computer-Vision Wear Analysis for Tillage Components

Developing a repeatable inspection pipeline that turns GoPro footage and laboratory measurements into quantitative wear profiles. The workflow combines controlled capture, DaVinci Resolve preprocessing, Fiji/ImageJ, OpenCV segmentation, paired-disc alignment, profile comparison, and zero-intercept quadratic trend fitting for industry-facing decisions.

Validated Digital Engineering of Fertilizer Flow

Owned the full simulation–experiment loop for granular machinery: material characterization, SolidWorks test-rig design, fabrication, Altair EDEM calibration, independent validation, and automated data reduction. All five validation targets were within the 15% engineering acceptance criterion, including 8.85% hopper-flow error and −1.43% projected-area error.
