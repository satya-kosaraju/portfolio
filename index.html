About

Who I am and what I’m working on.

I’m a graduate student in Agricultural & Biosystems Engineering at Iowa State University. My research focuses on Discrete Element Method (DEM) modeling of dry fertilizer, calibration using angle of repose, and multi-method validation with hopper discharge and impact plate tests.

I enjoy building the bridge between experimental data and simulation — tuning DEM parameters, validating models, and turning complex granular flow behavior into usable insights for machinery design.

DEM Simulation Workflow

From physical fertilizer to calibrated, validated DEM model.

01

Material Characterization

Particle size distribution, shape, density, friction, and restitution measured to define input material properties.

02

Calibration (AOR)

Static angle of repose experiments used to tune DEM contact parameters until the simulated heap matches physical behavior.

03

Validation

Independent setups—hopper discharge and impact plate tests—verify that the DEM model generalizes beyond calibration.

04

Spreader Simulation

Full spinning-disk spreader simulations predict distribution patterns and inform machine settings for uniform application.
