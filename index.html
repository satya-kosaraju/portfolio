About

Who I am and what I'm working on.

I'm a Master's researcher in Agricultural & Biosystems Engineering at Iowa State University, working at the Soil Machine Dynamics Laboratory in collaboration with John Deere. My thesis, "Optimization of Dry Fertilizer Spreaders: DEM-Based Material Flow Analysis and Experimental Validation", focuses on DEM modeling of urea, calibration via angle of repose, and multi-method validation with hopper discharge and impact plate tests.

I enjoy building the bridge between experimental data and simulation — tuning DEM contact parameters, validating models, and turning complex granular flow behavior into usable insights for machinery design.

DEM Simulation Workflow

From physical fertilizer to calibrated, validated model.

01

Material Characterization

Particle size distribution, shape, density, friction, and restitution measured to define input material properties.

02

Calibration (AOR)

Static angle of repose experiments used to tune DEM contact parameters until the simulated heap matches physical behavior.

03

Validation

Independent setups — hopper discharge and impact plate tests — verify that the model generalizes beyond calibration.

04

Spreader Simulation

Full spinning-disk spreader simulations predict distribution patterns and inform machine settings for uniform application.

2023 – 2026 · ISU × John Deere

DEM Calibration & Multi-Method Validation of Urea

Calibrated an EDEM model of urea fertilizer using angle of repose, then validated independently with hopper discharge and impact plate tests. Evaluated simulation accuracy using percentage relative error, confidence intervals, and repeatability analysis.
